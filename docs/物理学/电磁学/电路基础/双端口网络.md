# 双端口网络

Two-Port Networks

<Img src="/物理学/电磁学/电路基础/fig-19-1.svg" alt="fig-19-1" />

A **two-port network** is an electrical network with two separate ports for input and output.

## Impedance Parameters

$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{V}_1 &= \mathbf{z}_{11} \mathbf{I}_1 + \mathbf{z}_{12} \mathbf{I}_2 \\[8pt]
\mathbf{V}_2 &= \mathbf{z}_{21} \mathbf{I}_1 + \mathbf{z}_{22} \mathbf{I}_2
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{z}_{11} &= \frac{\mathbf{V}_1}{\mathbf{I}_1} \bigg|_{\mathbf{I}_2 = 0}, &\mathbf{z}_{12} &= \frac{\mathbf{V}_1}{\mathbf{I}_2} \bigg|_{\mathbf{I}_1 = 0} \\[8pt]

\mathbf{z}_{21} &= \frac{\mathbf{V}_2}{\mathbf{I}_1} \bigg|_{\mathbf{I}_2 = 0}, &\mathbf{z}_{22} &= \frac{\mathbf{V}_2}{\mathbf{I}_2} \bigg|_{\mathbf{I}_1 = 0}
\end{aligned}
$$

- $\mathbf{z}_{11} =$ Open-circuit input impedance
- $\mathbf{z}_{12} =$ Open-circuit transfer impedance from port 2 to port 1
- $\mathbf{z}_{21} =$ Open-circuit transfer impedance from port 1 to port 2
- $\mathbf{z}_{22} =$ Open-circuit output impedance

## Admittance Parameters

$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{I}_1 &= \mathbf{y}_{11} \mathbf{V}_1 + \mathbf{y}_{12} \mathbf{V}_2 \\[8pt]
\mathbf{I}_2 &= \mathbf{y}_{21} \mathbf{V}_1 + \mathbf{y}_{22} \mathbf{V}_2
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{y}_{11} &= \frac{\mathbf{I}_1}{\mathbf{V}_1} \bigg|_{\mathbf{V}_2 = 0}, &\mathbf{y}_{12} &= \frac{\mathbf{I}_1}{\mathbf{V}_2} \bigg|_{\mathbf{V}_1 = 0} \\[8pt]
\mathbf{y}_{21} &= \frac{\mathbf{I}_2}{\mathbf{V}_1} \bigg|_{\mathbf{V}_2 = 0}, &\mathbf{y}_{22} &= \frac{\mathbf{I}_2}{\mathbf{V}_2} \bigg|_{\mathbf{V}_1 = 0}
\end{aligned}
$$

- $\mathbf{y}_{11} =$ Short-circuit input admittance
- $\mathbf{y}_{12} =$ Short-circuit transfer admittance from port 2 to port 1
- $\mathbf{y}_{21} =$ Short-circuit transfer admittance from port 1 to port 2
- $\mathbf{y}_{22} =$ Short-circuit output admittance

## Hybrid Parameters

$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{V}_1 &= \mathbf{h}_{11} \mathbf{I}_1 + \mathbf{h}_{12} \mathbf{V}_2 \\[8pt]
\mathbf{I}_2 &= \mathbf{h}_{21} \mathbf{I}_1 + \mathbf{h}_{22} \mathbf{V}_2
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{h}_{11} &= \frac{\mathbf{V}_1}{\mathbf{I}_1} \bigg|_{\mathbf{V}_2 = 0}, &\mathbf{h}_{12} &= \frac{\mathbf{V}_1}{\mathbf{V}_2} \bigg|_{\mathbf{I}_1 = 0} \\[8pt]
\mathbf{h}_{21} &= \frac{\mathbf{I}_2}{\mathbf{I}_1} \bigg|_{\mathbf{V}_2 = 0}, &\mathbf{h}_{22} &= \frac{\mathbf{I}_2}{\mathbf{V}_2} \bigg|_{\mathbf{I}_1 = 0}
\end{aligned}
$$

- $\mathbf{h}_{11} =$ Short-circuit input impedance
- $\mathbf{h}_{12} =$ Open-circuit reverse voltage gain
- $\mathbf{h}_{21} =$ Short-circuit forward current gain
- $\mathbf{h}_{22} =$ Open-circuit output admittance

## Inverse Hybrid Parameters

$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{I}_1 &= \mathbf{g}_{11} \mathbf{V}_1 + \mathbf{g}_{12} \mathbf{I}_2 \\[8pt]
\mathbf{V}_2 &= \mathbf{g}_{21} \mathbf{V}_1 + \mathbf{g}_{22} \mathbf{I}_2
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{g}_{11} &= \frac{\mathbf{I}_1}{\mathbf{V}_1} \bigg|_{\mathbf{I}_2 = 0}, &\mathbf{g}_{12} &= \frac{\mathbf{I}_1}{\mathbf{I}_2} \bigg|_{\mathbf{V}_1 = 0} \\[8pt]
\mathbf{g}_{21} &= \frac{\mathbf{V}_2}{\mathbf{V}_1} \bigg|_{\mathbf{I}_2 = 0}, &\mathbf{g}_{22} &= \frac{\mathbf{V}_2}{\mathbf{I}_2} \bigg|_{\mathbf{V}_1 = 0}
\end{aligned}
$$

- $\mathbf{g}_{11} =$ Open-circuit input admittance
- $\mathbf{g}_{12} =$ Short-circuit reverse current gain
- $\mathbf{g}_{21} =$ Short-circuit forward voltage gain
- $\mathbf{g}_{22} =$ Short-circuit output impedance

## Transmission Parameters

$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{V}_1 &= \mathbf{A} \mathbf{V}_2 - \mathbf{B} \mathbf{I}_2 \\[8pt]
\mathbf{I}_1 &= \mathbf{C} \mathbf{V}_2 - \mathbf{D} \mathbf{I}_2
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{A} &= \frac{\mathbf{V}_1}{\mathbf{V}_2} \bigg|_{\mathbf{I}_2 = 0}, &\mathbf{B} &= - \frac{\mathbf{V}_1}{\mathbf{I}_2} \bigg|_{\mathbf{V}_2 = 0} \\[8pt]
\mathbf{C} &= \frac{\mathbf{I}_1}{\mathbf{V}_2} \bigg|_{\mathbf{I}_2 = 0}, &\mathbf{D} &= - \frac{\mathbf{I}_1}{\mathbf{I}_2} \bigg|_{\mathbf{V}_2 = 0}
\end{aligned}
$$

- $\mathbf{A} =$ Open-circuit voltage ratio
- $\mathbf{B} =$ Negative short-circuit transfer impedance
- $\mathbf{C} =$ Open-circuit transfer admittance
- $\mathbf{D} =$ Negative short-circuit current ratio

## Inverse Transmission Parameters

$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{V}_2 &= \mathbf{a} \mathbf{V}_1 - \mathbf{b} \mathbf{I}_1 \\[8pt]
\mathbf{I}_2 &= \mathbf{c} \mathbf{V}_1 - \mathbf{d} \mathbf{I}_1
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{a} &= \frac{\mathbf{V}_2}{\mathbf{V}_1} \bigg|_{\mathbf{I}_1 = 0}, &\mathbf{b} &= - \frac{\mathbf{V}_2}{\mathbf{I}_1} \bigg|_{\mathbf{V}_1 = 0} \\[8pt]
\mathbf{c} &= \frac{\mathbf{I}_2}{\mathbf{V}_1} \bigg|_{\mathbf{I}_1 = 0}, &\mathbf{d} &= - \frac{\mathbf{I}_2}{\mathbf{I}_1} \bigg|_{\mathbf{V}_1 = 0}
\end{aligned}
$$

- $\mathbf{a} =$ Open-circuit voltage gain
- $\mathbf{b} =$ Negative short-circuit transfer impedance
- $\mathbf{c} =$ Open-circuit transfer admittance
- $\mathbf{d} =$ Negative short-circuit current gain
