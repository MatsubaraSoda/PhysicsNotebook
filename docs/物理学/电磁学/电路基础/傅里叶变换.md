# 傅里叶变换

## 非周期函数

对于非周期函数，我们可以将其视为周期趋近于无穷大的周期函数。

<Img src="/物理学/电磁学/电路基础/fig-18-2.svg" alt="Effect of increasing T on the spectrum of the periodic pulse trains in Fig. 18.1(b) using the appropriately modified Eq. (17.66)." />

**不确定性原理 (Uncertainty Principle)**

不确定性原理指出，一个信号的时域和频域的宽度满足以下关系：

$$
\Delta t \Delta \omega \geq \frac{1}{2}
$$

其中，$\Delta t$ 是信号的时域宽度，$\Delta \omega$ 是信号的频域宽度。

这个关系表明，一个信号的时域和频域的宽度不能同时任意小。

## 定义

**正变换**：

$$
\boxed{
F(\omega) = \mathcal{F}[f(t)] = \int_{-\infty}^\infty f(t) e^{-j\omega t}\,\mathrm{d} t
}
$$

**逆变换**：

$$
\boxed{
f(t) = \mathcal{F}^{-1}[F(\omega)] = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{-\infty}^\infty F(\omega) e^{j\omega t} \,\mathrm{d} \omega
}
$$

<details>
<summary onmouseover="this.style.cursor='pointer'">推导过程</summary>

考虑傅里叶级数的指数形式

$$
f(t) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} c_n e^{j n \omega_0 t}
$$

其中

$$
c_n = \frac{1}{T} \int_{-T/2}^{T/2} f(t) e^{-jn\omega_0 t} \,\mathrm{d}t
$$

基频为

$$
\omega_0 = \frac{2\pi}{T}
$$

相邻频率的间隔为

$$
\Delta \omega = (n+1)\omega_0 - n \omega_0 = \omega_0 = \frac{2\pi}{T}
$$

于是有

$$
\begin{aligned}
f(t) &= \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} \left[ \frac{1}{T} \int_{-T/2}^{T/2} f(t) e^{-jn\omega_0 t} \,\mathrm{d}t \right] e^{jn\omega_0 t} \\[8pt]

&= \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} \left[ \frac{\Delta \omega}{2\pi} \int_{-T/2}^{T/2} f(t) e^{-jn\omega_0 t}\,\mathrm{d}t \right] e^{jn\omega_0 t} \\[8pt]

&= \frac{1}{2\pi} \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} \left[ \int_{-T/2}^{T/2} f(t) e^{-jn\omega_0 t} \,\mathrm{d}t \right] e^{jn\omega_0 t} \Delta \omega

\end{aligned}
$$

如果令 $T \to \infty$，则有

$$
\begin{aligned}
\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} &\quad \Rightarrow \quad \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \\[8pt]

\Delta \omega &\quad \Rightarrow \quad \mathrm{d} \omega \\[8pt]

n \omega_0 &\quad \Rightarrow \quad \omega \\[8pt]
\end{aligned}
$$

整理得到

$$
f(t) = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} 
\underbrace{\left[ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(\tau) e^{-j\omega \tau}\,\mathrm{d} \tau \right]}_{\displaystyle F(\omega)}
e^{j\omega t} \,\mathrm{d} \omega
$$

## 性质

### Linearity

$$
\mathcal{F}[a_1 f_1(t) + a_2 f_2(t)] = a_1 F_1(\omega) + a_2 F_2(\omega)
$$

### Time Scaling

$$
\mathcal{F}[f(at)] = \frac{1}{|a|} F\left(\frac{\omega}{a}\right)
$$

### Time Shifting

$$
\mathcal{F}[f(t-t_0)] = e^{-j\omega t_0} F(\omega)
$$

### Frequency Shifting

(or Amplitude Modulation)

$$
\mathcal{F}[f(t) e^{j\omega_0 t}] = F(\omega - \omega_0)
$$

### Time Differentiation

$$
\mathcal{F}\left[\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}t} f(t)\right] = j\omega F(\omega)
$$

and

$$
\mathcal{F}\left[\frac{\mathrm{d}^n}{\mathrm{d}t^n} f(t)\right] = (j\omega)^n F(\omega) \\[12pt]
$$

### Time Integration

$$
\mathcal{F}\left[\int_{-\infty}^t f(\tau)\,\mathrm{d}\tau\right] = \frac{F(\omega)}{j\omega} + \pi F(0) \delta(\omega)
$$

### Reversal

$$
\mathcal{F}[f(-t)] = F(-\omega) = F^*(\omega)
$$

### Duality

$$
\mathcal{F}[f(t)] = F(\omega)\quad \Rightarrow \quad \mathcal{F}[F(t)] = 2\pi f(-\omega)
$$

### Convolution

Recall:

$$
y(t) = h(t) * x(t) = \int_{-\infty}^\infty h(\tau) x(t-\tau) \,\mathrm{d}\tau
$$

If $X(\omega)$, $H(\omega)$, and $Y(\omega)$ are the Fourier transforms of $x(t)$, $h(t)$, and $y(t)$, respectively, then:

$$
Y(\omega) = \mathcal{F}[h(t) * x(t)] = H(\omega) X(\omega)
$$
